# v1.0.6 Tax Ownership, CGT and Share Returns Design

**Date:** 2026-07-14

**Release identity:** v1.0.6, displayed as `v1.06`

**Status:** Approved conversational design, awaiting written-spec review

## Goal

v1.0.6 improves the simulator's per-person tax allocation and makes share-related value and tax cash flows internally consistent. It assigns taxable other income to its owner, nets and carries forward capital losses per person, separates CGT funding from retirement income, and adds simplified share-price growth, dividends, and Australian franking credits.

This remains an educational projection. It does not determine legal ownership, eligibility for franking benefits, an individual's actual tax return, or professional tax advice.

## Approved Boundaries

### Included

- Other-income ownership using Person 1, Person 2, or Joint 50/50.
- Schema migration that preserves the existing 50/50 result.
- Per-person capital gains and capital-loss carry-forward within the projection.
- Loss application before the CGT discount.
- Scheduled full share sales and partial named-source share sales.
- CGT paid as a separate asset expense rather than deducted from retirement income.
- Per-holding expected nominal share-price growth, explicitly excluding dividends.
- Simplified cash dividends, ownership allocation, franking gross-up, tax offsets, and eligible refunds.
- Inline share income and franking fields inside the existing chevron-controlled holding editor.
- Conditional chart, table, CSV, event, and documentation output.

### Excluded

- Custom other-income ownership percentages.
- Opening carried-forward capital-loss inputs; both people start the projection at zero.
- CGT calculations for generic Other assets.
- Capital losses on personal-use assets, collectables, exemptions, rollovers, indexation, small-business concessions, and foreign-resident rules.
- Automated enforcement of the holding-period, related-payments, dividend-washing, residency, and other franking-integrity rules.
- Dividend-growth assumptions, dividend payment schedules, dividend reinvestment plans, and stochastic or market-sourced return forecasts.
- Taxation of super benefits before age 60.

## Data Model

### Other income

Every `otherIncome` item gains:

```js
owner: 'p0' | 'p1' | 'joint'
```

New items default to `joint`. Schema-10 items migrate to `joint`, exactly preserving the previous taxable 50/50 allocation.

### Shareholdings

Every shareholding gains:

```js
priceGrowthPct: number         // -100..100; nominal capital growth, excluding dividends
dividendYieldPct: number       // 0..100; cash yield on time-weighted value
frankedPct: number             // 0..100; portion of the cash dividend franked
companyTaxRatePct: 25 | 30     // rate used to derive the attached credit
frankingEligible: boolean      // explicit user assumption; defaults false
```

Existing and new holdings default to zero price growth, zero dividend yield, zero franked percentage, a 30% company tax rate, and `frankingEligible: false`. These defaults leave existing projections unchanged.

### Projection state

The projection state gains:

```js
capitalLossCarryForward: [number, number]
```

Both values start at zero. They are projection state, not editable scenario inputs.

### Share-sale record

Scheduled and partial share sales emit one common internal record:

```js
{
  holdingId,
  owner,
  proceedsNominal,
  allocatedCostBaseNominal,
  capitalResultNominal,   // signed: gain or loss
  discountEligible,
  saleMonth,
  saleKind                // scheduled | named-source
}
```

No sale function clips a loss to zero or applies the discount. Partial sales allocate cost base in proportion to the quantity sold.

## Per-Person Tax Ledger

Each year builds a ledger for each person with:

- ordinary taxable income;
- cash dividends;
- attached eligible franking credits;
- non-discount capital gains;
- discount-eligible capital gains;
- current-year capital losses;
- opening and closing carried-forward losses;
- losses applied to each gain bucket;
- net capital gain after losses and discount;
- ordinary income tax before the net capital gain;
- total tax after the net capital gain and franking offsets;
- incremental CGT; and
- estimated refundable franking offset.

The ledger is an internal calculation boundary with explicit inputs and outputs. Sale, dividend, tax, chart, table, and event code consume the ledger result rather than recomputing tax fragments independently.

## Capital-Loss Ordering

For each person and year:

1. Aggregate undiscounted gains and losses from that person's ownership share of every share sale.
2. Add current-year losses to the opening carried-forward balance.
3. Apply available losses to non-discount gains first.
4. Apply remaining losses to discount-eligible gains.
5. Apply the 50% individual CGT discount to the remaining eligible gains.
6. Add non-discount gains and discounted gains to obtain the net capital gain.
7. Carry any unused loss balance into the next year.

This ordering models the required loss-before-discount sequence and uses the loss allocation that generally minimizes the projected net capital gain. The methodology must state that real tax outcomes can require additional categories and choices outside this model.

## Other-Income Ownership

Taxable other income is allocated with the existing ownership weights:

- Person 1: 100% / 0%
- Person 2: 0% / 100%
- Joint 50/50: 50% / 50%

Non-taxable other income remains a household cash flow. Its stored owner value does not affect tax or cash flow while `taxable` is false.

## Share Growth, Dividend and Franking Calculation

### Share-price growth

At the start of each projected year, before dividends and share sales, every unsold holding's price changes by:

```text
opening share price × (1 + expected price growth %)
```

This matches the simulator's existing annual growth convention for super and Other assets. A sale in that projected year uses the grown price regardless of sale month; the month continues to control dividend exposure and sale-proceeds interest. The methodology must disclose this annual timing approximation.

Price growth:

- compounds independently for each holding;
- excludes cash dividends, preventing total-return double counting;
- changes market value, Age Pension assessable assets, dividend amount, sale proceeds, and capital gain or loss;
- does not change the holding's tax cost base; and
- stops after a holding is fully sold.

The interface may display expected total return as the derived sum of price growth and cash dividend yield, but it is not a separate editable assumption.

### Cash dividend

The annual cash dividend is:

```text
time-weighted grown nominal share value × annual cash dividend yield
```

The projection uses the quantity held during each month to calculate time-weighted value. A scheduled or partial sale reduces dividend exposure after its nominated sale or lump-sum month. This is an annual planning approximation, not an ex-dividend payment schedule.

Cash dividends:

- are allocated to people by share ownership;
- enter household income and the savings balance;
- appear as a separate chart funding source; and
- are excluded after the holding is fully sold.

### Franking credit

For the cash dividend's franked portion:

```text
franking credit = franked cash dividend × company tax rate / (1 - company tax rate)
```

When `frankingEligible` is true, the owner's assessable income includes the cash dividend plus the attached credit, and an equal tax offset is applied after income tax and Medicare calculations. The offset may produce an estimated refund. When eligibility is false, no attached credit is grossed up or claimed.

The UI and methodology must say that eligibility is a user assumption and that the simulator does not test the holding-period or other integrity rules.

## Tax and CGT Cash Flow

For each person:

1. Calculate tax on ordinary taxable income, including grossed-up eligible franked dividends but excluding the net capital gain.
2. Apply the person's franking offset, allowing an eligible excess offset to become an estimated refund.
3. Recalculate with the net capital gain and the same franking offset.
4. Define incremental CGT as the non-negative difference between the two results.

Household guaranteed income uses ordinary tax or refund only. It does not subtract incremental CGT, so a sale cannot create an artificial income-target top-up.

Share-sale proceeds are an asset transfer:

- scheduled proceeds enter savings;
- named-source proceeds fund the nominated lump sum;
- neither form satisfies the annual retirement-income target.

Incremental CGT is paid from savings. If savings cannot cover it, the remaining tax expense follows the configured household drawdown order in a separate funding pass. That funding reduces assets, does not count as income, and is recorded in the year's event data. Any unfunded remainder is displayed as a tax-funding shortfall rather than allowing a negative asset balance.

## Left-Panel Interface

### Other income

Each other-income editor adds one `Tax owner` selector with Person 1, Person 2, and Joint 50/50 options. It is disabled when `Taxable income` is off.

### Shareholdings

The selected layout is fully inline inside each existing expanded, chevron-controlled shareholding editor. After the existing owner and value fields, show:

1. Expected share-price growth %/yr, excluding dividends
2. Annual cash dividend yield %
3. Franked portion %
4. Company tax rate: 25% or 30%
5. Assume owner is eligible to claim franking credits

The collapsed summary remains compact. When yield is non-zero it adds a short suffix such as:

```text
BHP · John · 3.0% growth · 4.5% yield · fully franked
```

Zero-value growth, dividend, and franking details are omitted from the collapsed row.

## Validation and Error Handling

Validation rejects:

- an unknown other-income owner;
- share-price growth outside -100..100;
- dividend yield or franked percentage outside 0..100;
- a company tax rate other than 25 or 30;
- a non-boolean franking-eligibility value; and
- a non-finite derived dividend, gain, loss, tax, refund, or carry-forward balance.

Invalid scenario input blocks projection and uses the existing accessible scenario-message channel. Derived-calculation failures must not silently coerce to zero. Asset balances may not become negative.
